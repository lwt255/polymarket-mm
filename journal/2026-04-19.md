## The fix — pre-fill leader re-verification (commit `a28d47a`)

User directive: "stop the bot, do option 3 — we need to *ensure* whenever things flip, we don't trade. I will gladly make less money if it means we avoid flip catastrophes."

Implementation:
- New constant `PRE_FILL_VERIFY_DELAY_MS = 1500`
- Injected between the TRADE decision and the executor call:
  1. Wait 1.5s after T-33 decision
  2. Re-fetch both up/down order books in parallel
  3. Recompute leader side (higher `bestBid`)
  4. **If leader != decision side → abort, log to `candidate-stack-aborts.live.jsonl`**
  5. **If leader still same side but ask > 75¢ cap → abort (stale price)**
  6. Otherwise fire the taker order normally
- Aborts logged to a separate file (not diag) so parity audit parsing still works
- Hoisted `appendFileSync` to top-level import, removed dynamic import

All failure modes abort (conservative): exact tie, API failure both also trigger abort. Safer to fire nothing than fire blind.

### Timing impact
- Old: T-33 decide → fill ~T-30 (3s network latency)
- New: T-33 decide → verify T-31.5 → fill ~T-28 (5s total)

Effective fill is 1.5s later. User asked whether that degrades edge.

## Does the 1.5s delay degrade edge? (answer: effectively no)

Wrote `pre-fill-verify-impact.py` to replay collector history under three scenarios:

Book is too slow on our filter universe to move meaningfully in 1.5s.

Decomposed P&L (28 days historical):

The verify pays for itself **218× over**. It's a free lunch as close as we get.

## Afternoon: high-WR strategy research

After the live bot started behaving cleanly (3 trades, 1 abort log, 2W/1L), user raised two questions worth systematic investigation:

1. **"Do high win-rate strategies work on our markets?"** (friend has a ~95% WR bot; user skeptical because one loss wipes many wins)
2. **"What about volatility? We haven't looked at it."**

This turned into a full strategy-discovery pass over 28 days of collector data. Turning over every stone.

### Step 1 — Band-level baseline

`price-band-edge.py`: compute WR, breakeven (ask + 7% winner fee), and edge margin for every T-33 leader across all price bands, no filter.

Key numbers (13,385 markets):

| Band | N | Avg Ask | WR | Breakeven | Edge | $/tr |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| <55¢ | 399 | 52.6¢ | 54.9% | 54.4% | +0.48pp | +$0.11 |
| **65-70¢** | **633** | **67.0¢** | **69.7%** | **68.6%** | **+1.03pp** | **+$0.07** |
| 80-85¢ | 834 | 82.0¢ | 80.0% | 83.1% | −3.11pp | −$0.19 |
| 90-93¢ | 897 | 91.1¢ | 90.1% | 91.6% | −1.56pp | −$0.09 |
| 99+¢ | 3,081 | 99.3¢ | 98.9% | 99.3% | −0.38pp | −$0.02 |

**Takeaway: dumb "buy at 85¢+" strategies all lose money.** Market-makers price favorites ~efficiently — WR lags breakeven by 0.4-3.1pp across all high-price bands. Blanket high-WR strategies are a dead end.

Also note: "+$0.07/trade" at 65-70¢ is the *band-average* baseline (everything in range, no filter). The candidate-stack filter's +$1/trade @ $10 stake comes from actively selecting *within* the band using the filter stack — 7× better than the band baseline. I briefly conflated these in conversation; correction went back to the user.

### Step 2 — Single-filter screen in high bands

`high-wr-strategy-search.py`: test a battery of features — `cross_agree_all`, `leader_stable_240s`, `depth_strong_2x`, `spread_tight_1c`, `prev_match_fav`, etc. — in 85¢+ bands. Report edges where actual WR beats breakeven.

**Winner emerged: cross-crypto agreement.**

Single-filter top 5 in 85-90¢:
| Filter | N | WR | Edge | $/tr |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| cross_agree_all | 333 | 91.6% | +3.59pp | +$0.21 |
| cross_agree_2plus | 612 | 90.2% | +2.25pp | +$0.13 |
| interval_15m | 148 | 89.9% | +2.14pp | +$0.12 |

`cross_agree_all` = all 3 *other* cryptos' previous resolutions matched the current leader. Polymarket prices each market in isolation; it doesn't bake in the cross-crypto correlation. This is ~$79 of edge over 28 days on the `cross_agree_2plus` signal alone.

### Step 3 — Volatility and structural features

User: "something we haven't tried is volatility — how violent the price is moving."

`high-wr-v2-volatility.py`: added bid-level vol features (std dev, jump count, momentum), depth variations, book-tightness bands.

Results:
- **Bid volatility alone (std of leader bid over T-90..T-33) did NOT show as a top single filter.** Thresholds were poorly calibrated (set at 0.5¢ for "low" / 2¢ for "high"), and the signal is noisy at favorite prices where movement is dominated by step-changes.
- **Momentum direction did**: `bid_rising_33 + cross_agree_all @ 85-90` → 239 trades, 93.3% WR, +5.34pp edge, +$0.31/tr.
- **Depth helped too**: `cross_agree_all + deep_bid_10k @ 90-93` → 140 trades, 97.1% WR, +5.50pp edge, +$0.30/tr.

### Step 4 — Comprehensive v3: Chainlink vol, inter-market regime, volume, train/test

User: "dig into everything, turn over all stones."

`high-wr-v3-comprehensive.py`: added:
- **Chainlink underlying volatility** (per-crypto+interval previous-candle `chainlinkMoveDollars`, binned by quantiles)
  - `cl_prev_calm` / `cl_prev_violent` (last 1 candle)
  - `cl_3candle_calm_avg` / `cl_3candle_violent_avg` (last 3)
- **Inter-market regime** at T-33
  - `all_cryptos_favorite_85` (all 4 cryptos have ask ≥ 85¢)
  - `all_cryptos_same_side` (all 4 leaders point same direction)
  - `all_cryptos_one_sided` (all 4 asks ≥ 95¢ — total squash)
- **Volume**: `volume_high`, `volume_low`
- **Calibrated bid-vol**: `lowvol_calibrated` / `highvol_calibrated` using actual distribution quartiles

Then: **time-ordered 60/40 train/test split** on the top pair strategies to detect overfitting.

**Headline finding — underlying volatility matters (user's intuition confirmed):**

`cl_3candle_violent_avg` (last 3 candles' avg |Chainlink move| ≥ 75th pctile) is a real signal when combined with cross-agreement.

Top train/test validated pairs (14 of 15 held out-of-sample):

| Strategy | Band | Total N | Train Edge | Test Edge | Test $/tr | Holds? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **cl_3candle_violent_avg + cross_agree_all** | 85-90 | 75 | +8.06pp | **+8.35pp** | **+$0.47** | ✓ |
| **cl_3candle_violent_avg + cross_agree_2plus** | 85-90 | 137 | +7.74pp | +8.06pp | +$0.46 | ✓ |
| cl_prev_calm + cross_agree_all | 85-90 | 104 | +4.45pp | +9.39pp | +$0.54 | ✓ |
| cl_prev_calm + us_daytime | 85-90 | 102 | +0.53pp | +12.09pp | +$0.69 | ✓ |
| cross_agree_all + volume_high | 90-93 | 127 | +5.09pp | +8.30pp | +$0.45 | ✓ |
| cross_agree_all + deep_bid_10k | 90-93 | 140 | +5.27pp | +5.96pp | +$0.33 | ✓ |
| bid_rising_33 + cross_agree_all | 85-90 | 239 | +6.63pp | +3.18pp | +$0.19 | ✓ |
| cross_agree_2plus + highvol_calibrated | 85-90 | 267 | +8.80pp | −0.55pp | −$0.03 | **✗** |

The one overfit was the `highvol_calibrated` bid-vol feature — it works on train, collapses on test. This matches the story that bid vol is noisy; Chainlink vol is the real signal.

### Two-regime insight (important)

**Both calm AND violent Chainlink regimes are edge-positive with cross-agreement.** Mid-vol is where Polymarket prices correctly. The edge is in regime extremes:

- **Calm + cross-agree**: favorite locks in because nothing is disturbing the trend
- **Violent + cross-agree**: directional momentum trends extrapolate past the market's pricing

This is a genuinely new insight — not something captured in the candidate-stack strategy.

## 💡 Insights & Observations

- **Best possible test of the monitoring framework**: less than 24 hours after writing the v4 scaling plan with its iteration loop, the very first real-time check produced exactly the kind of signal the plan was designed to handle gracefully. Hot streak yesterday → audit found bugs → fixes confirmed sim is faithful → next day the strategy mean-reverts to baseline. The plan held up under live conditions immediately.
- **The Apr 7 hot streak and Apr 8 cool stretch are the same coin from two angles.** Both are normal variance for a 67% strategy at small sample sizes. Yesterday's +$199 day was a +2 SD outlier; today's mid-day 61% WR stretch is roughly a -1 SD draw. Average them and you get baseline. That's the whole shape of the strategy.
- **Mean reversion this fast is actually reassuring.** If the WR had stayed at 78% for a week, it would be more suspicious of an undiscovered bug, not less. Strategies that "always work above expectation" almost always have hidden errors. Strategies that revert to their stated baseline within a day or two are doing exactly what statistical theory says they should.
- **The discipline rules are written for moments like this.** Today's action (do nothing, check abort criteria, log the observation, move on) is almost mechanical because the plan exists. Without the plan, the temptation would be to either (a) "investigate" the cool stretch to find what's wrong, or (b) "adjust" the filter to capture more of yesterday's edge. Both would be mistakes. Plan-based discipline > intuition-based reaction.

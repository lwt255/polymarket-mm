### Balance Tracking Issues
Multi-trade candles cause noisy per-trade balance reads — when 2 trades fire on the same candle, both costs come out before either redeems, making individual actual PnL unreliable. First-to-last comparison ($68.20 → $75.25) is the accurate measure. Individual trade max loss is still $10 — the -$19 entries in the ledger are measurement artifacts.

## Session 2: Bug Fixes & VPS Setup (Late Night Apr 4 → Apr 5)

### Phantom Fill Bug (CRITICAL — found and fixed)
`executeWithFallback()` had a false positive: after cancelling a maker order, it checked `getOpenOrders()` — cancelled orders also disappear, but code assumed "gone = filled." Three session trades reported FILLED but never executed. Fixed by removing the "filled during cancel" assumption and always proceeding to taker fallback.

### L1 Bracket System Disabled
Bot still had L1 bracket logic running (entries at T-180, stop/target tracking). These were noise — never executing real trades, just cluttering logs. Removed entirely. Bot is now purely 9-signal v4.

### Gas/Fees Analysis
$0.13/trade friction = 1.3% on $10 trades. At $100/trade would be 0.13% — negligible. Gas is fixed cost that doesn't scale with trade size. The real answer to fees is scaling up, not reducing fees.

### Key Insights

**Is it variance or edge decay?**
Neither fully. The WR didn't gradually decay — it was rock-steady at 67% for 54 trades then hit one cold streak. But even at 67%, the strategy was barely breaking even before fees due to the asymmetric payoff (wins ~$4.50, losses ~$9.60).

**The catch-22 of thin-edge strategies:**
- The corrected backtest showed +$0.42/trade, -$0.13 fees = +$0.29 net
- You need ~500 trades to statistically prove a $0.29 edge is real
- But surviving 500 trades at $10/trade requires $300-500 bankroll
- Can't justify the capital without proof, can't get proof without the capital

**Why the collector showed profit but live didn't:**
1. No execution costs in backtests ($0.13/trade friction)
2. Backtest assumes every qualifying trade is taken — bankroll limits which trades the bot actually executes
3. Some signal correlations may be overfitting (4-signal and 6-signal buckets)
4. 72 trades is within the confidence interval of both a +$0.42 edge AND a $0.00 edge

**How people actually win on Polymarket:**
- Market makers (earn spread, don't predict outcomes)
- Genuine information edge on political/event markets ($5-10/trade edge, not $0.29)
- Arbitrageurs (mechanical, not predictive)
- The crypto 5-min markets are close to efficiently priced — the favorite-longshot bias exists but is nearly fully captured by the pricing

### Decision: Paper Trade via Collector
Rather than risk more capital on an unproven edge, let the collector run for 500+ qualifying candles. Track what the bot *would have* traded. If the edge holds at +$0.40/trade over 500 trades, come back with proper bankroll ($500+). If it drifts to zero, saved hundreds finding out for free.

### Why "Previous Same" Filter Works (Momentum Theory)

The "prev same as underdog" filter is a **momentum signal**. When BTC drops in one window and the current underdog is also DOWN:
- Traders in the new window think "it already dropped, it'll bounce" → price UP as 60-70% favorite
- But short-term crypto trends persist → BTC keeps dropping → DOWN wins
- The market overweights mean-reversion when it should weight momentum

Stronger on 15m (62% vs 40%) because a 15-minute trend is more established than a 5-minute blip.

### Where Losses Come From

1. **Trend reversal**: Momentum worked 3 times, we bet on #4, but the trend ended. Unavoidable — momentum works until it doesn't.
2. **Market was correctly priced**: Favorite at 65% actually was 65% likely. We bet against it and lost. The market isn't always wrong.
3. **Late-window shifts**: Price looked mispriced at T-120s but conditions changed in the last 30 seconds.

Losses cluster when trends reverse — the prev-same filter keeps betting with momentum until the trend ends, causing 2-3 consecutive losses. This is the normal 4-5 loss streak pattern.

**Why it still works**: Buying at 25-35¢, getting paid $1 on wins. Break-even is ~30% win rate. Our 42% gives a 12% margin above break-even — that's the profit.

## Session 4: Strategy Philosophy & Position Sizing

### Why Early Exits / Trailing Stops Don't Work Here

Binary markets have a **fixed $1.00 payout**. Unlike stocks or crypto spot trading where price can keep going up (unlimited upside), the max payout is capped. This means:

- Buy at 30¢, hold to resolution, win = **+70¢/share**
- Buy at 30¢, sell at T-30s when price is 60¢ = **+30¢/share** (cut winner in half)
- Buy at 30¢, price drops to 15¢, panic sell = **-15¢ realized loss** (could have still won at resolution)

Trailing stops work in markets with unlimited upside (stocks, futures, forex). In binary markets, the optimal play is always hold to resolution. Data confirmed: hold = $4,686, smart exit = $4,025. Every exit strategy tested left money on the table.

### More Trades vs Fewer Trades

**More trades wins.** This strategy has positive expected value per trade (~$4-10). Each individual trade is uncertain, but over 100+ trades the math converges. Like a casino — they don't win every hand, but they play thousands of hands.

The cross-asset cooldown reduces trade count, which is a cost. But it prevents correlated double-losses, which is worth it. Slightly fewer trades with better risk management beats maximum trades with blowup risk.

### Position Sizing: Fixed, Not Compounding

**Don't compound with a 42% win rate strategy.**

- **High win rate (80%+)**: Compounding works because drawdowns are rare. Win, add winnings, win again on bigger base.
- **Our 42% win rate**: Losing streaks of 3-5 are normal. If you compound to $140 and hit a streak, you lose $7/trade instead of $5. Drawdowns feel bigger, temptation to quit rises.

**The right approach: Fixed position sizing with step-up validation.**

| Stage | Trade Size | Requirement to Advance |
|---|---|---|
| Testing | $5/trade | 50+ trades, profitable |
| Level 1 | $10/trade | 50+ trades, profitable |
| Level 2 | $25/trade | 50+ trades, profitable |
| Level 3 | $50/trade | Ongoing |

Each step validates the strategy at that size before risking more. Profits accumulate as a cushion, not as fuel for bigger bets. If any level stops working, you catch it early at small size.

**Mental model:** "I put up $100. I bet $5 every trade. The $100 is my runway. Profits sit in the account as safety margin. I don't touch the trade size until I've proven it works over 50+ trades."

### Key Principles for Live Trading

1. **Volume is the edge** — more trades = more certain the math works out
2. **Fixed sizing** — don't compound, don't size up after wins or down after losses
3. **Hold to resolution** — no trailing stops, no profit taking, no panic selling
4. **Step up slowly** — $5 → $10 → $25 → $50, each level validated with 50+ trades
5. **The strategy wins through patience** — any single trade is a coin flip, but 100 trades is a business

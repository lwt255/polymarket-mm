### Session 5 — Health watchdog + monitoring

**Layer 1 — Local health shortcut (`v4-health`):** SSHes to VPS, runs `v4-health-check.py`, prints clean health summary with NO P&L info. ## Key Decisions

1. **Trailing stop $25 from peak** — locks in high-water marks, prevents the "wake up to evaporated gains" problem. EV cost is minimal (only bites when a session genuinely bounces back from a deep drawdown).

2. **Profit sweep alerts at $20 steps** — requires manual withdrawal + bot restart to rebaseline. Could automate later but manual is safer.

3. **Full dataset correction** — three wrong filters reverted (dead hours, BTC-DOWN, ETH 65-74). One new filter added (BTC 65-74). The corrected stack is validated on 1,759 baseline trades across 24 days, not 324 trades across 7 days.

4. **Health watchdog over auto-restart** — alert-only monitoring. User explicitly doesn't want auto-restart (burned before). The watchdog's job is to catch broken states and push a notification, not to fix them.

5. **"Check health, not P&L"** — the v4-health shortcut deliberately surfaces only mechanical state (service, process, log, errors, trail cushion) and zero financial data. Designed to break the habit of tailing logs and feeling every trade.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Check the data span before running "all-time" analyses.** The 7-day VPS tail silently passed as "full collector data" and produced three wrong filter decisions. Saved as `feedback_check_data_span_first.md`.

2. **Sim edges are not live targets.** The "+$1.75/tr" number was wrong (7-day artifact), but even the corrected "+$0.86/tr" is an in-sample sim number. Don't cite it as a benchmark. Saved as `feedback_stop_citing_sim_target.md`.

3. **Cut weak live buckets, don't "see if they work."** When sim shows marginal edge and live shows concentrated losses, cut — don't defend the sim number with real money. Saved as `feedback_real_money_vs_sim.md`.

4. **Selection bias in attention.** "Every time we go live, things go wrong" is an attention pattern, not a statistical one. Wins during analysis time are invisible; losses during watching time hit full force. The strategy doesn't know when you're watching.

5. **The edge is real.** Despite all the filter mistakes and data confusion, the underlying strategy (buy leader in 54-75¢, confirm with sigs≥2, hold to resolution) holds up across 1,759 trades spanning 24 days. The overnight loss was an execution-layer failure (wrong filters), not an edge failure. This distinction matters for confidence.

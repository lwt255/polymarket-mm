## Session 3: Market Making Deep Dive + Morning vs Afternoon Analysis

### Market Making Revisited — Still Dead

Re-investigated market making with 15 days of data. Key findings:

**Pure MM**: 0% of trades had both bid and ask fill. Always stuck holding one side to resolution. -$4,174/day. Catastrophic.

**Spread capture** (buy T-120, sell T-30): Negative P&L. Price moves 10-20¢ per candle but spread is only 1-2¢. Sell winners early, hold losers.

**Hybrid exit** (sell at 80-90¢ before resolution): No improvement. We buy at 54-75¢ at T-30, there's no time for price to reach exit threshold before binary resolution.

**Informed maker** (what we're doing): +56% over taker at 100% fill rate. This IS our structural edge.

### Why Market Makers Win and We Can't Replicate It

1. They're on BOTH sides simultaneously — collect $1.02 for $1.00 exposure, profit regardless of outcome
2. They don't hold to resolution — cancel and re-quote constantly
3. Sub-second execution — pull quotes before adverse selection hits
4. Inventory management across markets — hedge exposure
5. We enter once and hold to binary outcome — 100% exposed

**Our counter-edge**: We wait for T-30 when outcome is most predictable. They have to provide liquidity the entire candle including uncertain early minutes. Patience and selectivity is our advantage.

### The Structural Edge Question

Casino edge = structural (built into rules, never decays). Our edge = informational (could decay as others find it). The most structural things we have:
- **Maker rebate** — exchange rule, not a pattern
- **T-30 leader asymmetry** — persists as long as market structure exists
- **Iteration speed** — ability to detect and adapt faster than edge decays

Day started -$47 (morning), ended +$46 (afternoon). Investigated why. Answer: **nothing measurable changed.**

**Conclusion**: This was randomness, not a pattern to optimize around. Over 1,500 collector trades, morning and afternoon are both 67-71% WR. Today front-loaded the losses. The law of large numbers in action — 21 trades is a coin flip, 39 starts to converge, 1,500 is reliable.

### Psychology of Trading Through Bad Stretches

The hardest part: trusting the numbers during a -$47 morning. The data says keep trading (every trailing stop/daily cap tested made things worse). The math is the same as a casino — they don't close the blackjack table after 10 winners. The edge is in the next 1,000 hands, not the next hand.

Key difference from a casino: our edge is informational, not structural. It could decay. That's why continuous monitoring (collector) and iteration matter. We're not guaranteed to win — we're guaranteed to *know* if we stop winning.

## Session 7: CRITICAL BUG — Phantom Trades in executeWithFallback

### The Bug
The `executeWithFallback()` method in `order-executor.ts` had a false-positive fill detection:
1. Place maker order at bid+1¢
2. Wait 12s — no fill
3. Cancel the order
4. Check if order is in open orders — it's NOT (because we just cancelled it)
5. **BUG**: Code assumed "not in open orders after cancel = filled during cancel"
6. Reports FILLED when the order was actually CANCELLED

This meant session 2's 3 "wins" (+$16.91) were phantom — no orders actually executed, no money moved. The balance read $76.91 before AND after every trade.

### How We Caught It
User noticed balance wasn't increasing despite 3 "wins." Investigation showed:
- `balanceBefore` and `balanceAfter` were identical ($76.91) for all session 2 trades
- All 3 trades had the pattern "Maker unfilled after 12s → Maker filled during cancel"
- A clean test trade (that genuinely filled in 1s) DID move real money (-$8.71 on a loss)

### The Fix
Removed the "filled during cancel" false positive. After cancelling, proceed directly to taker fallback instead of assuming fill. The taker order at the ask will cross the spread and fill immediately — which is the safe path.

### Impact
- Session 1 trades (7 trades, 6W/1L) — money DID move, but changes were from the snipe bot running simultaneously, not our trades. Even session 1 trade #4 showed $0 balance change.
- Only trades that genuinely filled during maker polling (not during cancel) were real.
- The test3 trade (SOL LOSS -$8.71) was a real trade — maker filled in 1s, balance dropped correctly.

### Proxy Investigation (Dead End)
Initially suspected proxy wallet misconfiguration. Checked all 3 proxy addresses — none had USDC allowances. But the EOA has unlimited allowance to the Exchange contract. EOA mode (no proxy args) is correct — confirmed by the snipe bot working with the same code for 5 days.

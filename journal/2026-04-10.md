### Late UTC evening - Time-of-day analysis across all 672 collector trades

**What I found**:
- Baseline across 672 qualifying trades: **70.3% WR, +$0.85/tr, +$573 total**.
- Average entry price is ~64.5¢ across every 3-hour UTC bucket — price is **not** the differentiator.
- Win rate varies by ~18 percentage points across buckets at the same price.
- **Two buckets have no edge**:
  - **12-14 UTC (8-10am ET)**: 64.6% WR at 64.5¢ — exactly breakeven (`-$0.11/tr` over 79 trades).
  - **18-20 UTC (2-4pm ET)**: 58.8% WR at 64.6¢ — **5.9 points below breakeven** (`-$0.92/tr` over 80 trades).
- Every other bucket prints +$1.16 to +$2.02/tr.

- Worst single hour: **18:00 UTC (2pm ET)**, -$1.95/tr, 52% WR over 21 trades.

**Mechanistic read**:
- The 9-signal filter is *time-blind*: it picks the same kind of setups (same price, same depth, same rising pattern) at all hours, but those setups have very different predictive power depending on when they occur.
- 18-20 UTC corresponds to the US cash-equities afternoon — the tail of the session when dealer gamma hedging and end-of-day positioning drive crypto via correlation. Crypto moves that look like momentum at T-30 get faded before expiry because they are synthetic rather than organic.

**What I changed**:
1. Added a dead-hour skip to `computeSignals()` in [src/scripts/crypto-5min/microstructure-bot.ts](/Users/levanielthompson/Documents/Projects/polymarket-mm/src/scripts/crypto-5min/microstructure-bot.ts). Any trade in 12:00-14:59 UTC or 18:00-20:59 UTC is skipped with reason `dead hour`.
2. Updated the startup banner to show `skip 12-14,18-20 UTC`.
3. Deployed to VPS and restarted.

**Expected impact**: Removing the two dead buckets lifts sim edge from +$0.85/tr to ~+$1.10/tr on ~470 trades.

- **XRP**: Low-signal like BTC (peaks at 2 sigs, +$3.23/tr). **Has a DOWN bias that mirrors BTC's UP bias** — XRP DOWN prints +$2.97/tr at 85% WR on 39 trades, while XRP UP is +$1.50/tr.

**Interpretation**:
- These are genuinely four different strategies. The 9-signal system is a one-size-fits-all detector of "something interesting in the book", but the *same* setup means different things in different cryptos:
  - BTC: directional momentum in a biased tape
  - ETH: mispricing in cheap markets
  - SOL: confluence-confirmed setups
  - XRP: retail overreaction on the DOWN side
- The "right" long-term architecture is probably a **per-crypto strategy stack** with crypto-specific signal weightings — but that requires 3-5x more data per crypto than we currently have.

**What I did NOT change**:
- No ETH 65-74¢ skip. It's breakeven-positive (+$0.25/tr), not losing. Cutting it saves nothing and drops volume; user priority is **stay profitable**, not trim for tidiness.
- No XRP directional filter. The DOWN bias is real but the 39-trade UP bucket still prints +$1.50/tr — cutting it is uncalled-for.
- No BTC time-of-day carve-out. 15-24 trades per sub-bucket is too thin to trust; the 100% WR on 06-08 UTC is clearly sample artifact.
- No sizing tweaks (user explicitly pushed back on being "too confident").

**Decision — stopping here with filter changes**:
- We are at **5 filters stacked** (zone, dead hours, sigs>=2, no BTC-DOWN, plus the existing two-sided/rising checks). Each one was earned with adequate sample, clear effect, and mechanistic story.
- **Further filters should come from out-of-sample live data, not another pass on the same 672-trade sim**. Every incremental cut from here increases overfit risk without clear reward.

### 8:30 PM — Built Realistic Execution Model

**Activity**: Identified that naive model assumes instant fill at best bid — built realistic simulator to get trustworthy numbers before going live again.

**Problem with Naive Model**:
1. Assumes instant fill at best bid — no slippage
2. No execution delay — book can change in 2-3s
3. No taker fees (0.5%)
4. No adverse selection (0.2%)
5. No gas costs for split tx (~$1.00)
6. No fill probability modeling — not every order fills
7. No partial fills

**What We Built**:

New file: `src/scripts/autoresearch/split-execution-model.ts`
- `fetchFullBook()` — complete order book, all levels
- `walkBook()` — simulate selling into book with slippage across price levels
- `computeFillProbability()` — 70% base, -10% per $100 order size
- `simulateSplitSell()` — full pipeline:
  1. Settlement delay (5s) → re-fetch book
  2. Execution delay (2.5s) → re-fetch book again
  3. Walk book with slippage
  4. Apply fill probability + partial fills (50-100%)
  5. Gas costs ($1.00 per split)

Modified: `src/scripts/autoresearch/split-bot.ts`
- Replaced naive instant-fill with `simulateSplitSell()` call
- Realistic P&L accounting:
  - Correct prediction: `netRevenue - gasCost`
  - Wrong prediction: accounts for unfilled shares resolving to $1 (reduces loss)
  - No fill: `-gasCost` only
- Summary now reports full execution cost breakdown

Modified: `src/scripts/autoresearch/split-types.ts`
- Added optional realistic fields to `SplitMarketResult` and `SplitDryRunSummary`
- Fully backward compatible with existing loop and evaluator

**Design Decisions**:
- Real `sleep()` calls for settlement/execution delays, then re-fetch live book — captures genuine market movement, no synthetic noise needed
- Seeded PRNG per market for reproducible fill outcomes
- Drop-in replacement — loop and evaluator need zero changes

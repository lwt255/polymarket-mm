## Session 2: L1 Live Testing — Bracket Strategy Dead

### Live Bracket Trade Tests

Ran two live bracket trades ($10 and $5) to test whether pre-placed sell limits work for L1 stops.

**Test 1 ($10, BTC DOWN @51¢):**
- Buy filled. Sell limit at 48¢ failed: "balance: 0, order amount: 19000000"
- Tokens hadn't settled on-chain yet. Held to resolution — LOSS -$9.69.
- Price dropped from 51¢ to 0¢ with no stop protection.

**Test 2 ($5, SOL UP @76¢):**
- Buy filled. Added 5s settlement delay + balance refresh. Sell limit at 73¢ placed successfully.
- **But: sell filled IMMEDIATELY at 73¢ even though bid was 67¢.**
- Lost -$0.24 on a trade that was going our way.

### Why Pre-Placed Sell Limits Don't Work

A SELL limit order on a CLOB fills instantly if ANY buyer exists at or above your sell price. Placing a sell at 73¢ when the bid is 67¢ means you're offering below market — instant match.

This is standard CLOB behavior, not a bug. There's no "only fill when price drops to X" mechanic.

### Polymarket Has No Native Stop Orders

Confirmed via SDK investigation:
- OrderType enum: only GTC, GTD, FOK, FAK. No STOP, STOP_LIMIT, or conditional orders.
- No stop or conditional REST API endpoints.
- `postOnly` flag exists but doesn't prevent crossing the spread.
- The ONLY way to implement a stop is poll-based: watch the bid, place sell when bid drops.

### Poll-Based Stops Don't Work Either

From the overnight L1 dry run (282 trades):
- Average slippage: 6.6¢ past stop level
- 41% of stops slipped >5¢
- 21% slipped >10¢
- Avg stop loss: -$1.46 (expected -$0.45 at -3¢ stop)
- **Every bracket configuration is negative with realistic slippage (0/14 winning days)**
- Only works with zero slippage (pre-placed sells), which we just proved doesn't work

### L1 Is Dead

The bracket price-trading strategy requires exact stop fills. Polymarket can't provide them:
- Pre-placed sell limits fill immediately (wrong direction)
- Poll-based stops have 6.6¢ avg slippage (too expensive)
- No native stop order support

The $433/day and $547/day projections were based on zero-slippage assumptions that are physically impossible on this exchange.

### L2 Resolution Strategy Is Working

While L1 failed, L2 had a perfect day:
- **10W/0L (100% WR), +$60 combined**
- All trades had 2+ signals (flip60, odd_flips, cross>=2)
- L2 doesn't need sell orders — it holds to resolution (binary outcome, guaranteed settlement)

### Lessons Learned

1. **Always test execution before trusting backtests.** Two $5 trades saved us from deploying a strategy that fundamentally can't work.
2. **Polymarket is a binary market, not a trading venue.** You can buy and hold to resolution, but you can't actively manage positions with stops.
3. **The resolution mechanic IS the product.** L2 works because it embraces binary outcomes rather than fighting them.
4. **Simple beats complex.** L2 (buy leader at T-30, hold to resolution) is more robust than L1 (buy early, manage position with brackets).

### Session 3 — CRITICAL: maker-first adverse selection discovered

User asked if anything can be done about the 19% miss rate. Pulled miss-WR stats expecting them to be "selectivity." Instead found:

**Unfilled trades: 45/49 = 91.8% would-have-won WR** (vs 65.3% on filled trades).

Classic maker adverse selection: the bot posts at bid+1¢ during T-30. When the signal is RIGHT and price rises, the ask walks away and we miss. When the signal is WRONG and price drops, our bid gets hit. Fills skew toward losers, misses toward winners.

Math check on taker-first:
- Avg `fillPrice` ($0.641) is already ≈ avg `underdogAsk` ($0.637) — most fills are falling through to taker after 12s timeout anyway
- So taker-first buys **speed**, not worse prices
- Estimated missed PnL across 389 lifetime trades: **+$173 net** ($213 from captured wins − $40 from captured losses)
- Expected benefit: fill rate 87% → ~98%, live WR converges to sim

**Decision**: deploy taker-first as `--taker-first` CLI flag (defaults off, preserves rollback). Kept `maxTakerPrice = 0.75` safety rail. Codex review deferred — user hit limits. Moved forward without it given strength of evidence.

### Session 10 — Hourly-market edge research (Binance historical backfill)

Didn't wait for Polymarket collector data. Instead, pulled historical Binance 1h candles directly — since the hourly markets resolve on candle close vs open, we can test any price-only signal against 365 days × 6 assets = 52k candles.

Built `src/scripts/hourly-signal-research.py`. Tested 5 hypotheses:

**1. Prior-direction mean reversion (full 365 days):**
Every asset shows UP-after-UP < UP-after-DOWN. Edges: BTC -2.34pp, BNB -2.91pp, ETH -2.79pp. Real but weaker than the initial 90-day window suggested (-4.24pp for BTC).

**2. Quarterly regime check — this is the catch:**

| Asset | Q1 Apr-Jul 2025 | Q2 Jul-Oct | Q3 Oct-Jan 2026 | Q4 Jan-Apr 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BTC | **+0.66pp** | -0.50pp | -5.26pp | -4.36pp |
| SOL | **+1.12pp** | -2.30pp | -1.14pp | -4.02pp |
| BNB | **+0.95pp** | -6.11pp | -4.20pp | -2.71pp |
| DOGE | **+0.74pp** | -3.80pp | -4.62pp | -0.75pp |

**Mean reversion only exists in late-2025 and 2026.** Q1 2025 (bullish) showed slight momentum in 4 of 6 assets. This means prior-direction betting is regime-dependent — works now, could flip when market returns to trending mode.

**3. Cross-asset amplification:** 48.2% next-BTC-UP after all-6-UP prior hour; 51.6% after all-6-DOWN. ~3.4pp gap. Weaker than the 90-day window showed (~4.8pp) but directionally consistent with mean reversion.

**4. Time-of-day bias — the real prize.** Regime-independent, statistically solid with n=365/hour:

Strong UP hours (convergent across assets):
- **UTC 21:00 (5PM ET)**: BNB +11.4pp, DOGE +7.5pp, SOL +6.7pp, ETH +5.6pp, BTC +4.8pp
- **UTC 22:00 (6PM ET)**: SOL +7.0pp, BNB +5.3pp, BTC +4.0pp
- **UTC 16:00 (12PM ET, US open)**: BNB +6.4pp, SOL +4.3pp

Strong DOWN hours (convergent):
- **UTC 23:00 (7PM ET)**: XRP -7.5pp, DOGE -5.3pp, BNB -5.1pp, SOL -3.7pp
- **UTC 13:00 (9AM ET)**: BTC -4.3pp, DOGE -4.0pp

5 assets all showing +4-11pp UP at 5PM ET is not noise — likely US equity close / 4PM ET futures settle / coincident fund flows.

**5. Streak continuation:** Mean reversion strengthens with longer streaks (BTC after 6-streak: 39.6% continuation). Same regime-dependency caveat.

## Strategy Hypothesis for Hourly Markets

**The 5m bot's 9 signals do NOT transfer** — confirmed opposite direction at 1h scale. Momentum at 5m becomes mean reversion at 1h.

**Primary edge candidate**: Time-of-day bias (regime-independent).
- Example: bet UP on BNB UTC 21:00 hourly → 61.4% historical WR
- Polymarket at 50¢ → +$0.11/share = 22% per trade EV (if market doesn't already price TOD)
- Convergence across 5 assets at UTC 21:00 gives multi-asset diversification

**Secondary filter**: use prior-direction mean reversion as a gate (skip UP trades when prior 3 hours were UP — streak reversion).

**Regime risk**: prior-direction signal is gated by market regime. TOD is not. Start with TOD-only strategy for simplicity.

**Next validation step (when hourly collector has 2-3 weeks of data):**
- Compare our historical TOD-based WR to actual Polymarket implied probability at those same hours
- If Polymarket consistently prices TOD correctly, there's no edge (market has already figured it out)
- If Polymarket under-prices the bias, we have a tradeable edge
